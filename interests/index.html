Interests

As you can probably guess from my major, I enjoy programming. I really enjoy algorithms and compete in different programming competitions including google code jam and the ACM ICPC programming competitions.

The classes I have taken on AI and computer vision have been very interesting, and I would like to learn more in those areas of computer science.

I also enjoy learning new programming languages and have fooled around with a bunch of esoteric programming languages.
A few examples:
    a calculator written in befunge
    a secret message written in brainfuck
    a prime number generator written in lolcode

I am also very interested in security testing. I have fooled around a bit with Damn Vulnerable Web App, and am currently trying to pass the final level of the Natas web game by overthewire.org. Both of these have taught me a lot about developing secure websites and applications.

Volunteer

I really enjoy doing volunteer work. The feeling of camaraderie that you get when you spend your free time working together with a group of people, coupled with the sense of accomplishment you experience when do something selfless is exquisite.

Currently I am spending my free time organizing and leading youth group at my church in my hometown while attending school. I am also a member of the Computer Science Students' Society at the University; I organize intramural sports teams and other social events for Computer Science Students.

This past summer I spent a couple weeks volunteering at Circle Square Ranch Bible Camp taking care of and training horses, and giving kids riding lessons.

Some of my previous volunteer experience includes being a member of 4H for many years, and travelling to Mexico on two missions trips that included building houses and running a daily bible school.

Personal

I am very passionate about sports. When I was very young I played hockey, but when I became a teenager I switched to taekwondo and and played on my school's soccer and basketball teams. I continued my involvement with hockey as a referee and began reffing soccer as well.

Just before I turned 17 I got my blackbelt in taekwondo. After I accomplished this major goal of mine, my involvement with martial arts dwindled and recently I have turned my focus to weightlifting noncompetitively.

I have also become very involved with intramural sports while in university, and for the last couple years I have been on 4 different teams each semester.

I played guitar a lot when I was younger. In fact, I was part of a rock band in high school that I received school credit for. We put on a concert at the end of each school year as a sort of final exam. I have not found much time to pursue my musical interests while in university, but would like to get back into it eventually.

I also dabble a bit in graffiti. I honed my skills by painting two walls in my parent's basement and I also have a work on display at my hometown church. I have not touched a lot of graffiti lately, but I did teach a couple art classes at my old high school recently.

Lastly, growing up on a cattle ranch I spent a lot of my childhood on horseback. I love horses and find a lot of similarities between training horses and programming computers. As a living being horses are a lot more complex and unpredictable than a computer (in some ways) but teaching a horse is not unlike teaching a computer. It just requires the proper language.
