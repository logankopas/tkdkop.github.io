Travel Blog

Europe 2015

I recently, as a graduation gift to myself, took a month to travel around Europe. The experience was incredible. It was a fantastic opportunity to discover many of the fascinating cultures across Europe. Coming from a small Canadian town, I was overwhelmed by the history each city has to offer. Before travelling I would have been hard pressed to even imagine the abundance of rich history in each city and country that I visited.
Partly for myself, and mostly for my family and friends that want to hear about my trip, I am creating this blog to document my trip. Hopefully I will be quick in getting it all up here, but 28 days is a long time, and it will be a slow process going through pictures, researching history and backstory, and formatting the website. I will upload progress as I go, so keep checking back as I continue posting my trip in chronoligical order.

Day 0: Saskatoon -> Toronto

The day had finally come, it was time to leave Saskatoon. I started the day off by going to the gym. I knew it would be the last time for a whole month. Marcus' and my flight was sometime in the afternoon, we left Saskatoon in the daylight and landed in Toronto just after sunset. This was the first time I had been to Eastern Canada; however, we only had a 3 hour layover so we didn't have time to explore the city. Hopefully I will have time for that later in the summer, as Marcus and I are thinking of going to a music festival in Montreal. After eating supper at a restaurant in the airport we caught our overnight flight to Heathrow.

Day 1: Toronto -> London, England

The overnight flight was long, and I have never been able to sleep in any sort of vehicle. Little did I know at that point how inconvenient that would be in the coming month. We landed at Heathrow Airport at around 2pm and got on the tube to travel to Russel Square. We didn't realize how far that was, and our enthusiasm quickly waned during the 45 min trip. At Leister Square the tube stopped because of a disturbance on the tracks and we decided, since we were using backpacks, that we could walk the rest of the way. We exited the metro station and were immediately faced with a difficult dilemma: which way do we look when we cross the street? We ended up getting plenty of time to figure that out as we got turned around a couple times and took a detour through China Town. We arrived at the hotel around 5 or so, and decided we needed to stay awake in order to adjust to the timezone. We visited the British Museum and then found a pub and ate supper. It took us a while to realize that table service isn't commonplace in English pubs and eventually figured out that we had to go up to the bar to order food. After eating we returned to the hotel, couldn't force ourselves to stay up any longer, and promptly passed out.

Day 2: London

I woke up the next day feeling refreshed. Marcus, however, was not so lucky. It ended up taking him a few days to adjust to the time change. In the following two weeks we were signed up for a Contiki bus tour, and so that evening we were scheduled to attend a meet-up/information session so we had all day to explore the city. We started by walking to Trafalgar square and then walked to Westminster Abbey. It was a saturday, so the line up for the Abbey was huge. We ended up just going in to St. Catherine's Chapel and then leaving. Then we walked to Buckingham Palace. We happened to arrive around noon and ended up seeing the changing of the guard, then we walked through St. James's Park. Then, we walked down to the river and saw the Eye of London and Big Ben. After that we stopped for some food and then went back to explore more of the British Museum. The most fascinating exhibit there was a display about clocks. We learned how clocks are made, and this ended up being beneficial as later in the trip we got to see many clock towers and even saw the inner mechanisms for a few. We spent enough time at the museum that it was then time to go to our contiki meet up. We met a few people at the meet up, then went to a nearby pub to get to know everyone a little better. We ended up talking to Phil, from Toronto, Mollie, from New York, and Doug and Sam, a couple from Ontario. After chatting for a while, we all went down to Piccadilly Circus and checked out the night life there. We walked around a bit, decided to go into a club, then 5 minutes later decided to go back to our hotel because the bus was leaving at 6 the next morning.

Day 3: London -> France -> Belgium -> Amsterdam, Netherlands

At 6am we got on the bus and began the first day of our contiki tour. Everyone was very excited despite the hour and our tour leader, Jake, managed to memorize everyone's name, all 53 of us, by the time we reached the ferry at Dover. After taking the ferry (a first for me) we all got back on the bus in France. During the bus trip through Belgium to Amsterdam we did a "speed dating" exercise to get to know everyone. Well, half of everyone. What I mean by that is that everyone in sitting in an aisle seat rotated to the next seat and got a couple minutes to meet the person they were sitting beside before moving to the next seat. That meant that I spoke to half of the people on the bus and Marcus spoke to the other half. - we arrived in amsterdam, river cruise, team building, etc.
